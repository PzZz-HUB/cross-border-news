      New EU customs duty now in effect for low-value imports

                               

      Starting July 1, 2026, the European Union is introducing a new customs duty of €3 on imported items valued under €150. To help ensure a smooth buying and selling experience, eBay has updated its import fee experience for eligible transactions and will continue to support cross-border trade into the EU. What this means for sellers  Due to these changes in EU customs requirements, USPS label purchases on eBay are temporarily unavailable for shipment to the EU. Sellers should purchase their shipping label directly from their carrier to complete their shipment. For sellers using eBay International Shipping (eIS), applicable import fees, including any customs duties, will continue to be handled as part of the program. Buyers will pay applicable import fees at checkout and should not be charged additional import fees at delivery. For sellers using their own shipping methods, carriers may collect applicable import fees as part of the import process. Some carriers may begin requiring duties-prepaid shipping, also known as Delivered Duty Paid (DDP), into the EU for certain services or destinations. Sellers should check with their shipping provider regarding any new EU requirements, and review their pricing and shipping arrangements, as appropriate. What this means for buyers For items shipped through eBay International Shipping (eIS), applicable import fees will be shown at checkout and buyers should not be charged additional import fees at delivery. For items shipped using a seller's own shipping method, carriers may collect applicable import fees as part of the import process. Depending on the shipping service used, buyers may be contacted by the carrier about applicable import fees before delivery. Delivery times may also be longer than usual during the initial implementation period as carriers and customs authorities adjust to the new requirements. What’s next? We will continue to monitor the implementation of the new EU requirements and provide updates as additional guidance becomes available. We encourage sellers to check back regularly for the latest information. As always, our goal is to make international trade as simple as possible. Please note that the €3 customs duty applies per unique item under the applicable EU customs rules. For more information, see the European Commission’s guidance page . Thank you for being part of the eBay community.

      Now Streaming - Proven Summer Marketing tricks

                       

      Summer Marketing Tactics | eBay for Sellers Webinar Recap Last week, eBay seller Chuck Van Pelt joined eBay Product Marketing Manager Evan Swanson and ProSeller Account Manager Alex Lobos to share proven  Summer Marketing Tactics at the latest eBay for Sellers Webinar.  The webinar focused on one central idea: consistently using Seller Hub marketing tools can increase conversions and drive long-term growth. Key strategies from the session are below, and the full session is available to watch on YouTube.    Key Takeaway The strongest sellers combine multiple marketing tools as a coordinated strategy. By leveraging Seller-Initiated Offers, Sale Events, Volume Pricing, Order Discounts, Coupons, and Newsletters together, sellers can improve conversion rates, move inventory faster, increase order values, and build long-term customer loyalty. Turn Buyer Interest Into Sales Faster Seller-Initiated Offers (SIO) help you engage buyers who have already shown interest. Recent enhancements to SIOs include: Offer negotiations integrated directly into buyer messaging Ability to resend expired offers through messages Extended 96-hour counteroffer windows Centralized offer management and automation controls in Seller Hub One standout insight: buyers who message a seller are 10x more likely to convert when they receive an offer. Sellers were encouraged to respond quickly, automate offers when possible, and focus on highly engaged buyers rather than broad discounting. Keep Inventory Moving Aging inventory ties up both cash and storage space. Rather than letting products sit indefinitely, sellers should reduce stale inventory by using Sale Events and targeted promotions. Recommended tactics include: Using "Days on Site" filters to identify older inventory Running short, focused sales rather than permanent storewide discounts Creating structured discount tiers to gradually move aging inventory Seller Chuck Van Pelt summarized the philosophy simply: "Run a store, not a storage facility." Increase Average Order Value Two key tools help drive larger purchases: Volume Pricing encourages buyers to purchase multiple quantities of the same item. This works especially well for consumables, parts, and everyday essentials that buyers often reorder. Order Discounts reward buyers for adding more items to their cart by offering percentage discounts or minimum purchase promotions. Chuck shared how offering discounts on multiple CDs significantly increases cart size, with combined shipping helping offset the cost of discounts. Build Loyalty and Repeat Purchases Coupons and Store Newsletters are effective tools for staying connected with buyers after the initial sale. Sellers can use these tools to: Reward loyal customers Re-engage past buyers Promote new inventory and seasonal sales Encourage repeat purchases Coupons can also serve as a customer service tool, helping turn potentially negative experiences into positive ones by demonstrating goodwill and encouraging future business. Test, Learn, and Optimize Speakers stressed that the most effective sellers continuously test different discount levels, promotions, and marketing strategies to determine what works best for their specific category and customer. The recommendation: start experimenting now so you know which tactics drive results before entering the critical Q4 holiday selling season.

      EU Right to Repair Directive: What business sellers need to know

               

      If you sell goods to customers in the EU, you may need to update your repair information and buyer communications to comply with new EU Right to Repair rules. Starting July 31, 2026, when a EU buyer contacts you about a faulty item and asks for a remedy, you must offer a choice of repair or replacement. If they choose a repair, your warranty period is extended by 12 months. This applies to all general goods. What you need to do For general goods: If you are a business seller offering goods to buyers in the EU, and a EU buyer invokes their statutory rights during the warranty period, you have the following responsibilities: If an item is reported as faulty or not as described, you must offer the buyer a choice between repair or replacement. If a buyer chooses a repair, the warranty period for the item is extended by an additional 12 months. You must also inform the buyer of this extension before you conduct a repair. We recommend reviewing and updating your warranty processes and all published policies to reflect this. Purchases made before July 31, 2026 are not subject to these requirements. Additional requirements for certain products listed in Annex II: For certain products, in the categories listed in Annex II of the Directive - including washing machines, washer-dryers, fridges, dishwashers, TVs, vacuum cleaners, tumble dryers, welding equipment, smartphones, tablets, cordless phones, servers and data storage products, and products that include batteries for light electric vehicles (such as e-bikes, e-scooters, and similar devices) - additional requirements may apply. These requirements apply if you are the EU-based manufacturer. If the manufacturer is outside the EU, someone in the EU must take over its repair obligation. This will be the manufacturer’s EU authorized representative, or if there is none, the importer, or if there is no importer, the distributor. For goods listed in Annex II Check the EU Ecodesign rules for your products to understand which spare parts, repair information, and services you must offer, and how long they must remain available. Offer repair services for the duration of your repair obligation. On eBay, publish repair information, including details about coverage, repair options, and typical repair prices. You can make this information available through seller notes, the item description field, and/or the order confirmation email. If Annex II repair obligations don’t apply to you, you may still want to help your buyers by informing them who they should contact for a repair. Thank you for selling on eBay.

      Launching easy boost: The fastest way to promote your items

      Save time and sell faster with easy boost   Easy boost is the fastest and easiest way to promote all of your items at once. Set a single ad rate and we’ll automatically help boost your items higher up in search results and across eBay. You can reach more buyers, get more visibility, and help sell your items faster. New listings are automatically promoted too, which means less work for you. Simply navigate to the Advertising dashboard   in the mobile app:   READY Tap easy boost SET Set your ad rate BOOST Sell your items faster!         As always, you pay only when your item sells . Turn off easy boost any time.   Get started today in the eBay mobile app!   Frequently Asked Questions   How is easy boost different from promoting individual listings? Instead of choosing items one at a time, easy boost promotes everything in your account automatically. Your new listings are automatically included too, which means less work for you.   How much does easy boost cost? There’s no upfront cost. You pay an ad fee only when your item sells. The ad fee you pay is based on the ad rate you set.   Can I choose which listings are promoted? No. Easy boost promotes all eligible listings in your account. This helps keep things simple and ensures new listings are promoted automatically.   What happens when I create a new listing? If easy boost is on, any new eligible listings you create will be promoted automatically – no extra steps required.   Can I change my ad rate after turning on easy boost? Yes. You can update your ad rate at any time from the easy boost banner on the Advertising dashboard in the eBay mobile app.   Can I turn off easy boost? Yes. You can turn off easy boost at any time. When turning off easy boost, you’ll have the option to stop promotion for all of your listings, or only new listings.   What happens to listings I was already promoting? If you already have listings promoted using a general strategy, they’ll be included in easy boost automatically and will use the single ad rate you select (replacing the prior ad rate).   How do I see how easy boost is performing? You can see impressions, clicks, sales, and return on ad spend (ROAS) from the Advertising dashboard in the mobile app.   Who can use easy boost? Easy boost is available to all sellers who have access to the eBay mobile app, and are eligible to advertise on eBay.
